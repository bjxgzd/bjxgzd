1. Information We Collect

Personal Information

We collect personal information that you voluntarily provide to us when you register on the Site, express an interest in obtaining information about us or our products and services, when you participate in activities on the Site, or otherwise when you contact us. Personal information may include, but is not limited to:

  • Name and job title
  • Contact information (email address, phone number, mailing address)
  • Company or organizational information
  • Professional interests and preferences
  • Communication preferences
  • User account information (if applicable)

Automatically Collected Information

In addition to the information you provide us directly, we may automatically collect certain information when you visit, use or navigate the Site. This information may include, but is not limited to:

  • Internet protocol (IP) address
  • Device and browser information
  • Operating system
  • Referring and exit pages
  • Geographic location (based on IP address)
  • Cookie information
  • Pages viewed and time spent on pages and the Site
  • Search terms used to find the Site

Google Play and App Store Compliance

For applications distributed on Google Play and the App Store, we comply with their respective privacy and data collection policies. We collect data in accordance with Google's Play Developer Policy and Apple's App Store Review Guidelines, ensuring that all data collection is transparent and justified.

2. How We Use Your Information

Purpose of Collection

We use the information we collect in various ways, including to:

  • Process and fulfill your requests, respond to your inquiries, and provide you with the information, products, and services you request
  • Provide, operate, and maintain our website and services
  • Communicate with you about products, services, and promotional offers, with your consent
  • Analyze how our website and services are used to improve our offerings and user experience
  • Monitor usage patterns and troubleshoot problems
  • Provide technical support and customer service
  • Personalize your experience based on your interests and preferences
  • Send important notices and administrative communications
  • Monitor and protect against fraudulent, abusive, or illegal activity
  • Enforce our Terms of Service and other policies

3. How We Disclose Your Information

Business Transfers

If we are involved in a merger, acquisition, sale of company assets, or bankruptcy, your personal information may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will notify you via email or a prominent notice on our Site of any change in ownership or use of your personal information.

Third-Party Service Providers

We may share your information with third-party service providers who perform services on our behalf, such as website hosting, payment processing, analytics, marketing, customer service, and email delivery. These third parties have access to your information only to perform these services on our behalf and are obligated not to use it for other purposes.

Legal Requirements

We may disclose your information if required by law or if we believe in good faith that such disclosure is necessary to comply with legal processes, protect against harm to the rights, property, or safety of ourselves or others, or protect personal safety.

4. Data Security

Security Measures

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures designed to protect the security of your personal information against unauthorized or unlawful processing, against accidental loss, destruction, or damage, and against any other unlawful forms of processing. These measures include:

  • Encryption of sensitive data
  • Access controls and authentication mechanisms
  • Regular security assessments and penetration testing
  • Employee training on data protection
  • Secure data storage and transmission
  • Regular backup and disaster recovery procedures

Monetization Platform Compliance

We adhere to the security and data protection requirements of major monetization platforms including Google AdMob, Facebook Audience Network, and AppLovin. We implement measures to ensure compliance with their respective security standards and guidelines.

5. Data Retention

Retention Period

We will retain your personal information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this Privacy Policy, unless a longer retention period is required or permitted by law. When we no longer need your personal information, we will securely delete or anonymize it.

Global Privacy Regulations

In compliance with global privacy regulations including GDPR and CCPA, we implement data minimization principles and regularly review and update our data retention policies to ensure they align with current legal requirements.

6. Third-Party Services

Analytics and Advertising

We may use third-party services such as Google Analytics and advertising platforms to help understand how visitors use our Site and to serve targeted advertising. These services may use cookies to collect information about your use of our Site.

  • Google Analytics: Google's Privacy Policy
  • Google AdSense: Google's Advertising Principles
  • Facebook Pixel: Facebook Data Policy

Integration Services

We may integrate with third-party services to provide enhanced functionality to our users. These integrations may involve data transfers to service providers who are located in different jurisdictions. We ensure that such transfers comply with applicable data protection laws.

7. International Data Transfers

Cross-Border Data Flows

Beijing Starlight Pivot Culture Communication Co., Ltd is based in China, and your information may be transferred, stored, and processed in China or other countries where we operate or where our service providers are located.

Adequacy and Safeguards

We implement appropriate safeguards to ensure that your personal information is adequately protected when transferred internationally. These safeguards may include standard contractual clauses approved by regulatory authorities, adherence to privacy frameworks, or certification programs.

8. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device by websites you visit. They are widely used by website owners to make their websites work efficiently and to provide reporting information.

How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for various purposes, including:

  • Remembering your preferences and settings
  • Analyzing how you use our Site
  • Providing personalized content and advertisements
  • Measuring the effectiveness of our advertising campaigns
  • Ensuring the security of our website

Managing Cookies

You can control how cookies are used on your device through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to refuse or accept cookies. However, if you choose to disable cookies, some parts of our Site may not function properly.

9. Children's Privacy

No Collection from Children

Our Site is not intended for use by children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personally identifiable information from children under 13. If you are a parent or guardian and you are aware that your child has provided us with personal information, please contact us so that we can delete such information.

COPPA Compliance

We comply with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and similar international laws designed to protect children's online privacy. We implement age verification mechanisms and obtain verifiable parental consent before collecting any personal information from children.

10. GDPR and European Users

Data Protection in Europe

For users in the European Economic Area (EEA), we process personal information in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). We are committed to protecting your rights and providing transparency about how we use your information.

Your Rights

As a data subject in the EEA, you have certain rights under the GDPR, including:

  • The right to access and obtain a copy of your personal information
  • The right to rectification of inaccurate personal information
  • The right to erasure (the "right to be forgotten")
  • The right to restrict processing
  • The right to object to processing
  • The right to data portability
  • The right to withdraw consent

Processing Legitimate Interests

In some cases, we may rely on our legitimate interests as a legal basis for processing your personal information. We ensure that our legitimate interests do not override your rights and freedoms. Where necessary, we conduct a balancing test to ensure this is the case.

11. CCPA and California Residents

Privacy Rights for California Residents

For California residents, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) provides additional rights regarding personal information. These rights include:

  • The right to know what personal information we collect about you
  • The right to delete your personal information
  • The right to opt-out of the sale or sharing of personal information
  • The right to non-discrimination for exercising your privacy rights

Do Not Sell My Personal Information

We do not sell personal information as defined by the CCPA. However, we may share information with service providers for business purposes. If you are a California resident and wish to opt-out of such sharing, please contact us using the contact information below.

12. Changes to This Policy

Significant Changes

In the event of material changes to our Privacy Policy, we will make a reasonable effort to notify you through prominent notices on our Site, via email, or other means before the change becomes effective. The date the revised Privacy Policy becomes effective will be indicated at the top of this policy.
